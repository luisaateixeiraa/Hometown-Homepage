Top three Activities to visit at Porto

Clérigos Tower

The Clérigos Tower, a Baroque masterpiece by Nicolau Nasoni from the 18th century, is one of Porto's most iconic landmarks. Its 240-step climb rewards visitors with stunning panoramic views of the city and the Douro River.

The Most Beautiful Bookstore in the World

Livraria Lello, carries over a century of heritage, blending its architectural space and book expertise into a unique experience for visitors. As one of the oldest bookstores in Portugal, it is an iconic landmark in Porto and a must-see for anyone visiting the city.

Homem do Leme Beach

With its 374 meters of rocky shoreline, is popular with families and offers amenities like two playgrounds. It was the first beach in Porto to receive the Blue Flag and also holds the Gold Beach and Accessible Beach titles.

Discover more about the city

Popular festival of St John

São João is one of the biggest festivals in Portugal and a highly anticipated event in Porto, celebrated on June 24, with festivities beginning the night before and lasting until dawn. Over time, various traditions have become integral to the celebration, such as hitting people with leeks, symbolizing fertility, and using lemon balm branches. Plastic hammers, a modern replacement, are also used to tap people's heads, creating loud sounds. The event features hot air balloons, fireworks over the Douro River, manjerico pots with poems, and the iconic "cascatas sanjoaninas," miniatures depicting the city's crafts and culture. Music and concerts fill the city throughout the day and night.

Dragon Stadium and FCP Museum

The Estádio do Dragão, home of FC Porto, offers more than just football matches. It also houses the award-winning FC Porto Museum, known for its high-quality collection and interactive features.
